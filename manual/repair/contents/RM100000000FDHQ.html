51442 1.REMOVE NO. 2 ENGINE UNDER COVER

a.

Remove the 4 bolts and No. 2 engine under cover.


2.DRAIN ENGINE COOLANT

CAUTION:

Do not remove the radiator cap while the engine and radiator assembly are still hot. Pressurized, hot engine coolant and steam may be released and cause serious burns.

Remove the radiator cap.


b.

Loosen the radiator drain cock plug to drain the engine coolant.

HINT:

Collect the engine coolant in a container and dispose of it according to the regulations in your area.



c.

Loosen the cylinder block drain cock plug to drain the engine coolant in the engine.


3.ADD ENGINE COOLANT

a.

Tighten the cylinder block drain cock plug.

Torque:
12.7 N*m (130 kgf*cm, 9 ft.*lbf)


b.

Tighten the radiator drain cock plug by hand.


c.

Add engine coolant.

Standard Capacity:
Heater Specification Specified Condition
w/o Heater 9.8 liters (10.4 US qts, 8.6 Imp. qts)
w/ Front Heater 10.6 liters (11.2 US qts, 9.3 Imp. qts)
w/ Front and Rear Heater 11.7 liters (12.4 US qts, 10.3 Imp. qts)


NOTICE:

Do not substitute plain water for engine coolant.


HINT:
·

TOYOTA vehicles are filled with TOYOTA SLLC at the factory. In order to avoid damage to the engine cooling system and other technical problems, only use TOYOTA SLLC or similar high quality ethylene glycol based non-silicate, non-amine, non-nitrite, non-borate coolant with long-life hybrid organic acid technology (coolant with long-life hybrid organic acid technology consists of a combination of low phosphates and organic acids).

·

The thermostat opening timing can be confirmed by pressing the No. 1 radiator hose by hand, and checking when the engine coolant starts to flow inside the hose.




d.

Slowly pour engine coolant into the radiator reservoir until it reaches the FULL line.


e.

Install the reservoir cap.


f.

Press the No. 2 radiator hose and No. 1 radiator hose several times by hand, and then check the engine coolant level.

If the engine coolant level is low, add engine coolant.


g.

Install the radiator cap.


h.

Start the engine and warm it up until the thermostat opens.

HINT:

The thermostat opening timing can be confirmed by pressing the No. 1 radiator hose by hand, and checking when the engine coolant starts to flow inside the hose.


NOTICE:

Immediately after starting the engine, if the radiator reservoir does not have any engine coolant, perform the following: 1) stop the engine, 2) wait until the engine coolant has cooled down, and 3) add engine coolant until the engine coolant is filled to the FULL line.



i.

Maintain the engine speed at 2000 to 2500 rpm.

NOTICE:
·

Make sure that the radiator reservoir still has some engine coolant in it.

·

Pay attention to the needle of the water temperature meter. Make sure that the needle does not show an abnormally high temperature.

·

If there is not enough engine coolant, the engine may burn out or overheat.

·

Run the engine at 2000 rpm until the engine coolant level has stabilized.




j.

Press the No. 1 radiator hose and No. 2 radiator hose several times by hand to bleed air.

CAUTION:
·

Wear protective gloves.

·

Be careful as the radiator hoses are hot.

·

Keep your hands away from the fan.




k.

Stop the engine, and wait until the engine coolant cools down to ambient temperature.

CAUTION:

Do not remove the radiator cap while the engine and radiator are still hot. Pressurized, hot engine coolant and steam may be released and cause serious burns.



l.

Check that the engine coolant level is between the FULL and LOW lines.

If the engine coolant level is below the LOW line, repeat all of the procedures above.

If the engine coolant level is above the FULL line, drain engine coolant so that the engine coolant level is between the FULL and LOW lines.


4.INSPECT FOR COOLANT LEAK
