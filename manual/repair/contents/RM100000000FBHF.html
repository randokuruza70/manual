2.ADJUST ENGINE IDLE SPEED AND MAXIMUM ENGINE SPEED

a.

Initial conditions.

i.

Engine at reach normal operating temperature.


ii.

Air cleaner installed.


iii.

All accessories switched off.


iv.

All vacuum lines properly connected.


v.

Valve clearance set correctly.


vi.

Injection timing set correctly.


vii.

Transmission in neutral position.



b.

Connect the tachometer.


c.

Adjust the engine idle speed.

i.

Check that the adjusting lever touches the engine idle speed adjusting screw when the accelerator pedal is released.

If not, adjust the accelerator linkage.


ii.

Start the engine.


iii.

Check the engine idle speed.

Standard engine idle speed:

600 to 700 rpm



iv.

Difference between each cylinder:

490 kPa (5.0 kgf/cm2, 71 psi) or less


If the cylinder compression in one or more cylinders is low, pour a small amount of engine oil into the cylinder through the glow plug hole and check the compression pressure for the cylinder with low compression.

HINT:
·

If adding oil helps the compression, the piston rings and/or cylinder bore may be worn or damaged.

·

If the pressure stays low, a valve may be stuck or the seating may be improper, or there may be leakage past the gasket.
