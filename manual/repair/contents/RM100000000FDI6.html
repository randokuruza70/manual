4.CHECK MAF AMOUNT

HINT:
·

While idling the engine, the EGR system operation can be checked by forcefully operating the EGR valve and checking the change in the MAF amount.

·

During normal idling of the engine, the EGR valve is approximately 60% open.

·

If the EGR valve is completely closed, the MAF amount increases.



a.

Start the engine, then run it at idle.


b.

Warm up the engine.

e.

Check the MAF reading.

HINT:

When the EGR valve connector of bank 1 or bank 2 is disconnected, the EGR valve of bank 1 and bank 2 are fully closed by the fail-safe function.


Standard:
Engine Condition Active Test Condition MAF Reading
Idling Normal condition
(Active Test not performed) 		6.1 to 26.5 g/sec.
EGR valve connector is disconnected
(Bank 1 and 2 are fully closed) 		14.2 to 26.5 g/sec.


HINT:
·

If the MAF reading is out of the standard range for either "EGR valve connector is disconnected" or "Normal condition", replace the EGR valve.

·

If the MAF reading is out of the standard range for both "EGR valve connector is disconnected" and "Normal condition", check the mass air flow meter. If any defects are found, replace the mass air flow meter, and then check the operation of the EGR valve.

5.VISUALLY INSPECT HOSES, CONNECTIONS AND GASKETS

a.

Visually check that the hoses, connections and gaskets have no cracks, leaks or damage.

NOTICE:
·

Detachment or other problems with the engine oil dipstick, filler cap, PCV hose and other components may cause the engine to run improperly.


·

Air suction caused by disconnections, looseness or cracks in the parts of the air induction system between the throttle body and cylinder head will cause engine failure or engine malfunctions.



If the result is not as specified, replace parts as necessary.
