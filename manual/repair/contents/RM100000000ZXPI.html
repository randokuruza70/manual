41204 1.INSPECT RUNOUT OF REAR DRIVE PINION COMPANION FLANGE REAR SUB-ASSEMBLY

a.
2.479,1.813 2.656,1.969 0.177,0.156 10 false *a
*a 35 mm (1.38 in.)


Using a dial indicator, measure the vertical and lateral runout of the companion flange.

Maximum Runout:
Runout Specified Condition
Vertical runout 0.10 mm (0.00394 in.)
Lateral runout 0.10 mm (0.00394 in.)


If the runout is more than the maximum, replace the companion flange.


2.INSPECT RUNOUT OF DIFFERENTIAL RING GEAR

a.

Using a dial indicator, measure the runout of the ring gear.

Maximum runout:

0.05 mm (0.00197 in.)


If the runout is more than the maximum, replace the ring gear with a new one.


3.INSPECT DIFFERENTIAL RING GEAR BACKLASH

a.

Using a dial indicator, measure the backlash of the ring gear.

Standard backlash:

0.10 to 0.20 mm (0.00394 to 0.00787 in.)


HINT:

Measure at 3 or more positions around the circumference of the ring gear.


If the backlash is not as specified, adjust the side bearing preload or repair as necessary.


4.INSPECT DRIVE PINION PRELOAD

a.

Using a torque wrench, measure the preload of the backlash between the drive pinion and ring gear.

Standard drive pinion preload (at starting):

0.89 to 1.41 N*m (10 to 14 kgf*cm, 8 to 12 in.*lbf)


If necessary, disassemble and inspect the differential.


5.INSPECT TOTAL PRELOAD

a.

Using a torque wrench, measure the total preload with the teeth of the drive pinion and ring gear in contact.

Standard total preload (at starting):

0.29 to 0.49 N*m (3 to 4 kgf*cm, 3 to 4 in.*lbf) + Standard drive pinion preload


If necessary, disassemble and inspect the differential.

a.

b.

a.

b.

Remove the shift fork shaft bolt.


c.

Using a screwdriver, pull out the differential lock shift actuator and remove the rear differential lock sleeve and rear differential lock shift fork.

b.

22.INSPECT RUNOUT OF DIFFERENTIAL CASE

a.

Place the 2 rear differential case bearings (outer race) on their respective bearings.

HINT:

Do not interchange the left and right outer races.



b.

Install the assembled plate washers onto the rear differential case bearing.


c.

Install the differential case onto the differential carrier.

HINT:

If it is difficult to install the differential case onto the carrier, replace the plate washer with a thinner one.


However, select a plate washer that allows no clearance between it and the carrier.


d.

Align the matchmarks on the bearing caps and differential carrier.


e.

Install and uniformly tighten the 4 bolts a little at a time.


f.

Using a dial indicator, measure the differential case runout.

Maximum case runout:

0.04 mm (0.00157 in.)


If the runout is more than the maximum, replace the differential case and side bearing as a set.


g.

Remove the differential case.
