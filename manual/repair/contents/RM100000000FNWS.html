HINT:

Collect the engine coolant in a container and dispose of it according to the regulations in your area.



b.

Remove the reservoir cap and using a wrench, remove the vent plug to drain the engine coolant.


c.

Slide the hose clamp and remove the engine air bleed cap.


d.

Loosen the engine drain cock plug to drain the engine coolant from the engine.


3.ADD ENGINE COOLANT

CAUTION:
·

Do not remove the reservoir cap while the engine and radiator assembly are still hot. Pressurized hot engine coolant and steam may be released and cause serious burns.

·

When pressing the radiator hoses:

·

Wear protective gloves.

·

Be careful as the radiator hoses are hot.

·

Keep your hands away from the radiator fan.




NOTICE:

Do not substitute plain water for engine coolant.


Total capacity:

14.8 liters (15.6 US qts, 13.0 Imp. qts)


a.

Tighten the radiator drain cock plug by hand.


b.

Tighten the engine drain cock plug.

Torque:
12.7 N*m (130 kgf*cm, 9 ft.*lbf)


c.

Add engine coolant to the radiator reservoir assembly, and press the No. 2 radiator hose and No. 2 water by-pass hose several times by hand to bleed air.

Capacity:

5.0 liters (5.3 US qts, 4.4 Imp. qts)


HINT:

TOYOTA vehicles are filled with TOYOTA SLLC at the factory. In order to avoid damage to the engine cooling system and other technical problems, only use TOYOTA SLLC or similar high quality ethylene glycol based non-silicate, non-amine, non-nitrite, non-borate coolant with long-life hybrid organic acid technology (coolant with long-life hybrid organic acid technology consists of a combination of low phosphates and organic acids).



d.

B Line


Add engine coolant to the radiator reservoir assembly until it reaches the "B" line.


e.

Using a wrench, install the vent plug.

Torque:
2.0 N*m (20 kgf*cm, 18 in.*lbf)

NOTICE:

Do not turn the vent plug to 5.0 N*m (51 kgf*cm, 44 in.*lbf) or more, as the vent plug will be damaged.



f.

Install the engine air bleed cap and slide the hose clamp to secure the cap.


g.

Install the reservoir cap.


h.

Bleed air from cooling system.

i.

Set the air conditioning as follows while warming up the engine.

Measurement Condition
·

Fan speed - Any setting except "OFF"

·

Temperature - Toward WARM

·

Air conditioning switch "OFF" (w/ Air Conditioning System)



ii.

Start the engine.

NOTICE:

Immediately after starting the engine, if the radiator reservoir assembly does not have any engine coolant, perform the following: 1) stop the engine, 2) wait until the engine coolant has cooled down, and 3) add engine coolant until the coolant is filled to the "FULL" line.




i.

Maintain an engine speed of 3000 rpm for approximately 10 minutes so that the thermostat opens and air bleeding is performed.

NOTICE:
·

Make sure that the radiator reservoir assembly still has some engine coolant in it.

·

Pay attention to the needle of the water temperature meter. Make sure that the needle does not show an abnormally high temperature.

·

If there is not enough engine coolant, the engine may burn out or overheat.

·

Make sure that the thermostat opens and that the engine coolant level has decreased.



HINT:

The thermostat opening timing can be confirmed by pressing the No. 2 radiator hose by hand, and checking when the engine coolant starts to flow inside the hose.



j.

Stop the engine and wait until the engine coolant cools down to ambient temperature.


k.

Check that the engine coolant level is between the "FULL" and "LOW" line.

If the engine coolant level is below the "LOW" line, repeat all of the procedures above.

If the engine coolant level is above the "FULL" line, drain engine coolant so that the coolant level is between the "FULL" and "LOW" line.
