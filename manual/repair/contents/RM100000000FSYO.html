iii.

From outside the vehicle, approximately 1 m (3.28 ft.) from the driver door, test the door control transmitter by pointing its key plate at the vehicle and pressing a door control transmitter switch.

OK:

The door lock can be operated via the door control transmitter.


·

The operational area differs depending on the user, the way the door control transmitter is held and the location.

·

The weak radio waves of the door control transmitter may be affected if the area has strong radio waves or electrical noise. The door control transmitter operation area may be shortened or the door control transmitter may not function.




b.

Inspect the transmitter battery capacity.

i.

Remove the transmitter battery from the door control transmitter that does not operate. Attach a lead wire (0.6 mm (0.0236 in.) in diameter or less including wire sheath) with tape or equivalent to the negative terminal.

NOTICE:

Do not wrap the lead wire around a terminal, wedge it between the terminals, or solder it. A terminal may be deformed or damaged, and the transmitter battery will not be able to be installed correctly.



ii.

Carefully pull the lead wire out from the position shown in the illustration and install the previously removed transmitter battery.
