POWER ASSIST SYSTEMS  VANE PUMP(except 1GR-FE)  INSPECTION  


PROCEDURE

1.CHECK OIL CLEARANCE BETWEEN VANE PUMP SHAFT AND VANE PUMP REAR HOUSING BUSHING

a.
2.563,0.24 2.323,0.24 2.323,0.24 2.094,1.104 false 2.188,1.302 2.427,1.823 2.427,1.823 1.99,1.313 true 2.625,0.135 2.927,0.375 0.302,0.24 10 false b 0.385,1.729 0.688,1.969 0.302,0.24 10 false a 2.49,1.792 2.792,2.031 0.302,0.24 10 false *a
*a Rear Housing Bush


Using a micrometer, measure the outer diameter [a] of the shaft.


b.

Using a vernier caliper, measure the inner diameter [b] of the rear housing bush.


c.

Calculate the oil clearance.

Oil clearance = b - a

Maximum oil clearance:

0.08 mm (0.00315 in.)


If the oil clearance exceeds the maximum, replace the vane pump assembly.


2.INSPECT VANE PUMP ROTOR AND VANE PUMP PLATES

a.

Using a micrometer, measure the thickness of the 10 vane pump plates.

Standard thickness:

1.405 to 1.411 mm (0.0554 to 0.0555 in.)


If the thickness is not within the specified range, replace the vane pump assembly.


b.
2.177,0.76 2.344,1.521 true 2.302,1.552 3.25,1.74 0.948,0.188 10 false *a
*a Feeler Gauge


Using a feeler gauge, measure the clearance between the side face of the vane pump rotor groove and the vane pump plate.

Maximum clearance:

0.03 mm (0.00118 in.)


If the clearance exceeds the maximum, replace the vane pump assembly.


3.INSPECT FLOW CONTROL VALVE

a.

Coat the flow control valve with power steering fluid and check that it falls smoothly into the front housing by its own weight.

NOTICE:

Do not damage the flow control valve or hole of the vane pump front housing.


If the flow control valve does not fall into the hole smoothly, replace the vane pump assembly.


b.

Check the flow control valve for leakage.

i.

Close one of the holes and apply 392 to 490 kPa (4.0 to 4.9 kgf/cm2, 57 to 71 psi) of compressed air into the opposite side hole, and confirm that air does not come out from the end holes.

If air leaks, replace the vane pump assembly.



4.INSPECT FLOW CONTROL VALVE COMPRESSION SPRING

a.

Using a vernier caliper, measure the free length of the flow control valve compression spring.

Minimum free length:

31.95 mm (1.26 in.)


If the length is less than the minimum, replace the vane pump assembly.


5.INSPECT PRESSURE PORT UNION

a.

Visually check the pressure port union for fluid leaks.

If there is a leak, replace the vane pump assembly.
