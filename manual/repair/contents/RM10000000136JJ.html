DESCRIPTION

To detect atmospheric pressure, the built-in atmospheric pressure sensor in the ECM is used for the common rail system. Following the changes in the atmospheric pressure, the ECM corrects the fuel injection volume, timing and duration, and adjusts the common rail internal fuel pressure in order to optimize engine combustion.

HINT:
·

When the engine switch is on (IG), the intake manifold pressure, "MAP", is approximately equal to atmospheric pressure.

·

Under standard atmospheric pressure conditions, the output of the atmospheric pressure sensor is 101 kPa. For every 100 m increase in elevation, pressure drops by 1 kPa. This varies by weather (high atmospheric pressure, low atmospheric pressure).

·

If DTC P2226, P2228 and/or P2229 is stored, the following symptoms may appear:

·

Misfire

·

Combustion noise

·

Black smoke

·

White smoke
