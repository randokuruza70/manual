1VD-FTV ENGINE CONTROL  ECD SYSTEM(w/o EGR System)  White Smoke Emitted  


DESCRIPTION

Faults and Symptoms of Common Rail Diesel Components


a.

Engine Control

Turbocharger System
Main fault Turbocharger sub-assembly malfunction (compressor side seal ring, turbine side seal ring, shaft, oil drain)
Symptoms White smoke
Excessive oil consumption

Glow System
Main fault Glow system malfunction
Symptoms Difficult to start, rough idle, knocking, white smoke (when cold)
Diagnostic Point
·

Measure the resistance of the glow plug

·

Check the glow plug indicator light



Engine
Main fault Loss of compression
Symptoms Rough idle (constant lack of power)
Data List Engine Speed of Cyl #1 to #8
·

When cranking during the "Check the Cylinder Compression" Active Test, if there is a high speed cylinder (approx. 100 rpm more than the other cylinders) that cylinder may lose compression.


Injection Feedback Val #1 to #8
·

If Injection Feedback Val #1 to #4 is more than 3 mm3/st, the cylinder may have a fault.




b.

Diesel Injection

Fuel injector
Main fault Blockage
Symptoms Rough idle, lack of power, black smoke, white smoke, knocking
Data List Injection Feedback Val #1 to #8
·

When an Injector Feedback Val is more than 3 mm3/st, there may be a malfunction in the corresponding cylinder. Injection Feedback Val #1 to #8 can be read after idling for 1 minute with the engine warmed up (the engine coolant temperature is higher than 75°C (167°F)).



HINT:

When the sliding resistance of the internal parts of the injector assemblies (nozzle needle, control valve) has increased due to internal contamination, injection quantity will increase at high fuel press due to a delay in fuel injector closure.


Injector Driver
Main fault Circuit fault: The fuel injector does not open.
Symptoms Difficult to start, rough idle, lack of power, black smoke, white smoke, knocking
Data List Same as fuel injector
Diagnostic Trouble Code When the injector driver has a fault, some DTCs may be stored.

Poor Quality Fuel
Main fault -
Symptoms Difficult to start, rough idle (especially when cold)

Explanation of Symptom


White Smoke When the engine is misfiring, any fuel that has been injected but remains unburned is emitted as white smoke.
Turbocharger internal oil leak (Click here (201701,999999,_51,_029760,_0309414,RM10000000136JT,))
If oil leak occurs from turbine side seal, large amount of white smoke will be emitted from the exhaust pipe.
Internal oil leak is not visible from the outside of the turbocharger sub-assembly.


PROCEDURE

1.CONFIRM CONDITIONS PRESENT WHEN WHITE SMOKE APPEARED WITH CUSTOMER
a.

Ask the customer about the conditions when the white smoke was emitted.

- What were the driving conditions when the white smoke was generated.

- Is the white smoke constantly emitted, or only occasionally?

- Does the white smoke occur only when the vehicle is cold, or when the vehicle is both cold and hot?

- Is the white smoke emitted just after starting the vehicle only, or does it continue while the vehicle is idling?


b.

Determine the symptoms of the problem to narrow down the possible causes.

·

Depending on whether there is oil mixed with the fuel, or whether there is unburned fuel present, the smell of the exhaust gas differs. When oil is mixed in, the exhaust gas smells such as burning oil.

·

When continuously traveling with the engine close to idle for 1 hour or more, such as when in heavy traffic, unburned fuel accumulates in the catalytic converter, and can produce white smoke when accelerating.

NOTICE:
·

When replacing the fuel injector for a cylinder, always be sure to use a new injection pipe.

·

Follow the procedure in the repair manual and temporarily install the No. 1 injection pipe sub-assembly, No. 2 injection pipe sub-assembly and No. 1 nozzle leakage pipe assembly, and then correctly position the injector assemblies. After that, tighten parts according to the torque specifications.

·

If the installation procedure is not performed correctly, injector assemblies may become out of position, which may cause the injector assemblies to deteriorate, resulting in malfunctions.

·

If an fuel injector deteriorates and malfunctions, other problems such as knocking, rough idle, etc. may occur.

·

If an fuel injector becomes out of position, it is possible that the seal between the fuel injector and injection pipe sub-assembly may become incomplete, resulting in a fuel leak.
