RM25T0E _51 Engine / Hybrid System _029758 1GR-FE ENGINE CONTROL _0135343 SFI SYSTEM J

1GR-FE ENGINE CONTROL  SFI SYSTEM  Fuel Pump Control Circuit  


DESCRIPTION

When the engine is cranked, an STA signal is sent to the ECM from ignition switch terminal ST1. When the STA and NE signals are received by the ECM, Tr1 inside the ECM turns on and current flows through the coil of the circuit opening relay. As a result, the relay switches on and the fuel pump operates with power from the fuel pump relay. While the NE signal is being sent (while the engine is running), the fuel pump continues to operate since Tr1 in the ECM continues to stay on (the circuit opening relay is on). The speed of the fuel pump is regulated at two speeds (high and low) according to the state of the engine (cranking, light load or heavy load). When Tr2 inside the ECM is off, the fuel pump operates at high speed. When Tr2 turns on during idling or light loads, current flows to the fuel pump through the fuel pump resistor and the fuel pump operates at low speed.

In the case that the vehicle is equipped with a sub fuel tank, when the STA signal and NE signal are input to the ECM, Tr1 turns on and current flows through the coil of the circuit opening relay. As a result, the relay switches on, power is supplied to the fuel pump via the fuel pump relay and pump select relay, and the fuel pump operates.

When the fuel tank select switch is off, the contact inside the pump select relay connects the power supply to the main fuel pump and the main fuel pump operates.

When the fuel tank select switch is on, the contact inside the pump select relay connects the power supply to the sub fuel pump and the sub fuel pump operates.
