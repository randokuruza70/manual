PROCEDURE

11401 1.CLEAN CYLINDER BLOCK SUB-ASSEMBLY

a.

Using a gasket scraper, remove all the gasket material from the top surface of the cylinder block sub-assembly.


b.

Using a soft brush and solvent, thoroughly clean the cylinder block sub-assembly.

NOTICE:

If the cylinder is washed at high temperatures, the cylinder liner sticks out beyond the cylinder block sub-assembly. Always wash the cylinder block sub-assembly at a temperature of 45째C (113째F) or less.



11401 2.INSPECT CYLINDER BLOCK SUB-ASSEMBLY

a.

Inspect for flatness.

i.

Using a precision straightedge and feeler gauge, measure the surfaces that contacts the cylinder head and main bearing cap for warpage.

Maximum warpage:

0.20 mm (0.00787 in.)


If the warpage is more than the maximum, replace the cylinder block sub-assembly.



b.

Visually check the cylinder for vertical scratches. If deep scratches are present, rebore all 6 cylinders. If necessary, replace the cylinder block sub-assembly.

4.CLEAN PISTON SUB-ASSEMBLY WITH PIN

a.

Using a gasket scraper, remove the carbon from the piston top.


b.

Using a groove cleaning tool or broken ring, clean the piston ring grooves.


c.

Using solvent and a brush, thoroughly clean the piston.

NOTICE:

Do not use a wire brush.

6.INSPECT PISTON PIN OIL CLEARANCE

a.

Inspect the piston pin fit.

i.

Heat the piston to approximately 80째C (176째F), and push the piston pin into the piston pin hole with your thumb.

If the pin can be installed at a lower temperature, replace the piston and pin as a set.



b.

Using a caliper gauge, measure the inside diameter of the connecting rod bush.

Standard bush inside diameter:

29.008 to 29.020 mm (1.1420 to 1.1425 in.)



c.

Using a micrometer, measure the piston pin diameter.

Standard piston pin diameter:

29.000 to 29.012 mm (1.1417 to 1.1422 in.)



d.

Subtract the piston pin diameter measurement from the bush inside diameter measurement.

Standard oil clearance:

0.004 to 0.012 mm (0.000157 to 0.000472 in.)


Maximum oil clearance:

0.030 mm (0.00118 in.)



7.INSPECT RING GROOVE CLEARANCE

a.

Inspect the ring groove clearance.

i.

No. 1 compression ring:

Using a feeler gauge, measure the clearance between a new piston ring and the wall of the ring groove.

Standard ring groove clearance:

0.054 to 0.095 mm (0.00213 to 0.00374 in.)


Maximum ring groove clearance:

0.20 mm (0.00787 in.)


If the clearance is more than the maximum, replace the piston.


ii.

No. 2 compression ring and oil ring:

Using a feeler gauge, measure the clearance between a new piston ring and the wall of the ring groove.

Standard Ring Groove Clearance:
Item Specified Condition
No. 2 compression ring 0.060 to 0.100 mm (0.00236 to 0.00394 in.)
Oil ring 0.030 to 0.070 mm (0.00118 to 0.00276 in.)


Maximum ring groove clearance:

0.20 mm (0.00787 in.)




8.INSPECT PISTON RING END GAP

a.

Insert the piston ring into the cylinder bore.


b.

Using a piston, push the piston ring a little beyond the bottom of the ring travel, 130 mm (5.12 in.) from the top of the cylinder block sub-assembly.


c.

Using a feeler gauge, measure the piston ring end gap.

Standard Piston Ring End Gap:
Item Specified Condition
No. 1 compression ring 0.270 to 0.370 mm (0.0106 to 0.0146 in.)
No. 2 compression ring 0.450 to 0.550 mm (0.0177 to 0.0217 in.)
Oil Ring 0.200 to 0.400 mm (0.00787 to 0.0157 in.)


Maximum Piston Ring End Gap:
Item Specified Condition
No. 1 compression ring 0.850 mm (0.0335 in.)
No. 2 compression ring 0.900 mm (0.0354 in.)
Oil Ring 0.880 mm (0.0346 in.)


If the end gap is more than the maximum, replace the piston ring. If the end gap is more than the maximum even with a new piston ring, rebore all 6 cylinders or replace the cylinder block sub-assembly.


13201 9.INSPECT CONNECTING ROD SUB-ASSEMBLY

a.

Using a rod aligner and feeler gauge, check the connecting rod alignment.

i.

Check for bend.

Maximum bend:

0.03 mm (0.00118 in.) per 100 mm (3.94 in.)


If the bend is more than the maximum, replace the connecting rod sub-assembly.


ii.

Check for twist.

Maximum twist:

0.15 mm (0.00591 in.) per 100 mm (3.94 in.)


If the twist is more than the maximum, replace the connecting rod sub-assembly.

13411 12.INSPECT CRANKSHAFT

a.

Inspect for circle runout.

i.

Place the crankshaft on V-blocks.


ii.

Using a dial indicator, measure the circle runout at the center journal.

Maximum circle runout:

0.06 mm (0.00236 in.)


If the circle runout is more than the maximum, replace the crankshaft.



b.

Inspect the main journals and crank pins.

i.

Using a micrometer, measure the diameter of each main journal and crank pin.

Standard Diameter:
Item Specified Condition
Main journal 66.982 to 67.000 mm (2.6371 to 2.6378 in.)
Crank pin 58.982 to 59.000 mm (2.3221 to 2.3228 in.)


If the diameter is not as specified, check the oil clearance. If necessary, replace the crankshaft.


ii.

Check each main journal and crank pin for taper and out of round as shown in the illustration.

Maximum taper and out of round:

0.02 mm (0.000787 in.)


If the taper and out of round is more than the maximum, replace the crankshaft.



c.

If necessary, grind and hone the main journals and/or crank pins.

i.

Grind and hone the main journals and/or crank pins to the finished undersized diameter.


ii.

Install new main journal and/or crankshaft pin undersized bearing.



13.INSPECT CHECK VALVE

a.

Push the valve with a wooden stick to check if it is stuck.

If stuck, replace the check valve.
