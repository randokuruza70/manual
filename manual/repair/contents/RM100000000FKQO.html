BRAKE CONTROL / DYNAMIC CONTROL SYSTEMS  ANTI-LOCK BRAKE SYSTEM  C146C  Open in ABS Motor Relay Circuit  C146D  Short in ABS Motor Relay Circuit  


DESCRIPTION

The ABS motor relay supplies power to the ABS pump motor. While the ABS is activated, the ECU turns the motor relay on and operates the ABS pump motor.

If the voltage supplied to the motor relay (+BM) is below the DTC's detection threshold due to low voltage from the battery or generator, the DTC may be stored.

DTC No. Detection Item DTC Detection Condition Trouble Area
C146C Open in ABS Motor Relay Circuit When either of the following is detected:
1.

All of the following conditions continue for 0.12 seconds or more.

·

The IG1 terminal voltage is between 9.5 and 17.2 V.

·

While initial check and ABS is operating.

·

Relay contact is open when the relay is ON.


2.

Both of the following conditions continue for 0.12 seconds.

·

The IG1 terminal voltage is less than 9.5 V.

·

Relay contact remains open when the relay is ON.



·

ABS MTR fuse

·

ABS motor relay circuit

·

Brake actuator assembly (ABS motor relay)


C146D Short in ABS Motor Relay Circuit When the motor relay is OFF, the motor relay remains closed for 4 seconds or more.
·

ABS motor relay circuit

·

Inspect the fuses for circuits related to this system before performing the following inspection procedure.

·

Before disconnecting the connector, make sure that there are no problems with the connection.

·

After disconnecting the connector, make sure that the connector case and terminals are not deformed or corroded.



HINT:

When C1241 is output together with C146C and/or C146D, inspect and repair the trouble areas indicated by C1241 first.
