CHECK FOR INTERMITTENT PROBLEMS


When intermittent problems occur in the emission control ECU signal circuits (NE, THW, PIM, VA) shown in the table below, the appropriate DTC is output.

Accordingly, when the DTCs shown in the table below (13, 22, 31, 41) are output during the DTC check, and an inspection of the appropriate circuits reveals no abnormality, check for intermittent problems as described below.

DTC Code Detection Item
13 Engine Speed Sensor Circuit Malfunction (NE Circuit)
22 Engine Coolant Temperature Sensor Circuit Malfunction
31 Vacuum Sensor Circuit
41 Throttle Position Sensor Circuit

By checking for intermittent problems, the place where intermittent problems are occurring due to poor connections can be isolated.

a.

Clear the DTCs.

b.

Perform a Simulation Test.

Using the simulation test, apply vibration to and pull lightly on the wire harness, connector and terminals in the circuit indicated by the trouble code.

In this test, if the glow indicator light turns on, it indicates that the place where the wire harness, connector or terminals being pulled and vibrated has a faulty connection. Check that point for loose connections, dirt on the terminals, a poor fit or other problems, and repair as necessary.

HINT:

After clearing the DTCs in memory, if the glow indicator light does not turn off after the engine is started, check thoroughly for faulty connections, etc., then try the check again. If the glow indicator light still does not turn off, check the emission control ECU and replace it if necessary.
