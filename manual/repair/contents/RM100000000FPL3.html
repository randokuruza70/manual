1VD-FTV STARTING  STARTER  INSPECTION  


PROCEDURE

28100 1.INSPECT STARTER ASSEMBLY

CAUTION:

As a large electric current passes through the cable during this inspection, a thick cable must be used. If not, the cable may become hot and cause injury.


NOTICE:

The following tests must be done within 3 to 5 seconds to prevent the coil from burning out.


a.

Perform operation test without load.

5.031,0.948 5.438,1.219 5.438,1.219 5.563,1.219 true 4.344,0.177 4.479,0.177 4.479,0.177 4.646,0.823 true 4.156,0.115 4.469,0.271 0.313,0.156 10 false *a 5.656,1.135 5.969,1.292 0.313,0.156 10 false *b
*a Terminal 50 *b Terminal 30


i.

Mount the starter assembly in a vise between aluminum plates.


ii.

Connect the battery and an ammeter to the starter assembly with terminal 30 disconnected as shown in the illustration.

NOTICE:

Do not connect terminal 30 to the battery.



iii.

Connect terminal 30 to the battery and check that the starter rotates smoothly and steadily while the pinion gear is moving outward. Then measure the current.

Standard current:

200 A or less at 11 V


If the result is not as specified, repair or replace the starter assembly.

c.

Using a vernier caliper, measure the commutator diameter.

Standard diameter:

36.0 mm (1.42 in.)


Minimum diameter:

35.0 mm (1.38 in.)


If the diameter is less than the minimum, replace the starter armature assembly.


d.

Using a vernier caliper, measure the undercut depth of the commutator.

Standard undercut depth:

0.7 mm (0.0276 in.)


Minimum undercut depth:

0.2 mm (0.00787 in.)


If the undercut depth is less than the minimum, correct it with a hacksaw blade.


e.

Check that the bearing rotates smoothly.

If necessary, replace the starter armature assembly.


f.

Inspect the commutator circle runout.

i.

Place the commutator on V-blocks.


ii.

Using a dial indicator, measure the circle runout.

Maximum circle runout:

0.05 mm (0.00197 in.)


If the runout is more than the maximum, correct it with sandpaper (#400) or replace the starter armature assembly.
