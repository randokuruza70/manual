CONSTRUCTION


a.

A compact and lightweight distributor type injection pump assembly is used.


b.

The following devices are used for the injection pump assembly depending on the model.

Device Function
Engine Speed Sensor Detects the engine speed.
High Altitude Compensator Compensates the injection volume in accordance with the altitude (atmospheric pressure).
Automatic Cold Start Device (ACSD) (Thermo Wax) Improves low temperature startability.
Throttle Position Sensor Detects the amount of pedal effort applied to the accelerator pedal.
Timing Control Valve (TCV) Optimizes advanced characteristics of the timer for partial load regions.
Boost Altitude Compensation System (BACS) Reduces black smoke which occurs due to sudden acceleration, etc., after having the EGR on.

c.

High Altitude Compensator*

*: Models with high altitude compensator


i.

The high altitude compensator consists of bellows, a spill ring and linkage (push rod, connecting pin, control arm and governor lever). Bellows which expand and contract according to the atmospheric pressure are used to adjust the spill ring position and control the fuel injection volume according to the atmospheric pressure. Performing this control increases fuel efficiency at high altitudes and reduces black smoke.

d.

ACSD*

*: Models with ACSD


i.

Engine coolant circulates around the ACSD (thermo wax), which has thermo wax enclosed. The thermo wax expands or contracts depending on the engine coolant temperature. This movement rotates lever "A" to rotate the roller ring. Lever "A" normally pushes the timer piston to the advanced position according to the force of the return spring. Also, the movement of lever "A" rotates the adjusting lever via lever "B".

OPERATION


a.

Basic Operation

i.

The centrifugal force type feed pump takes up a fixed volume of fuel with each turn. The fuel expulsion pressure from the feed pump is controlled by operation of the pressure regulator valve.


ii.

Fuel is sent from the outside of the feed pump, through the hole in the upper part of the feed pump cover and into the pump body.


iii.

The pump plunger also rotates as it moves up and down. After the fuel is drawn in, it is distributed under pressure to the delivery valve of each cylinder according to the injection order.


iv.

The centrifugal force type all speed governor installed in the upper part of the injection pump assembly moves the spill ring obstructing the plunger spill port and controls the fuel injection volume.


v.

The hydraulic type timer installed in the lower part of the injection pump assembly is operated by the fuel pressure of the pump housing, and movement of the roller ring by the timer advances the injection angle and controls the injection timing.


vi.

The fuel cut solenoid valve is connected to the ignition switch circuit, and when the ignition switch is turned off, the current is cut off, the solenoid is de-energized, fuel is cut off and the engine stops.

b.

High Altitude Compensator

i.

When Driving on Level Surface

·

Since the bellows do not expand and the spill ring is at the normal position, the fuel injection volume is normal.



ii.

When Driving at High Elevations

·

The bellows expand, moving the push rod to move the spill ring in the fuel injection volume reduction direction via the linkage. This movement causes the fuel injection volume to decrease.

c.

ACSD

i.

When the engine coolant temperature is low, the thermo wax contracts and lever "A" rotates in the clockwise direction by the force of the return spring. Since the force of the return spring is stronger than the force of the timer spring, lever "A" moves the roller ring and timer piston inside the injection pump assembly in the advanced direction while at the same time pushing the adjusting lever via lever "B" to perform idle up.

ii.

When the engine coolant temperature rises, the thermo wax expands, rotating lever "A" in the counterclockwise direction. This rotation makes the timer spring push and move the timer piston in the retarded direction, rotate the roller ring in the retarded direction and cancels the movement in the advanced direction when the engine starts. At the same time, the adjusting lever moves to its original position and cancels idle up.
