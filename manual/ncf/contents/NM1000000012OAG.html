TIRE PRESSURE MONITORING  TIRE PRESSURE WARNING SYSTEM  GENERAL  GENERAL  


OUTLINE


a.

A direct-sensing type tire pressure warning system is used for some models.


b.

The direct-sensing type tire pressure warning system directly detects the tire pressure via tire pressure warning valve and transmitters installed in each disc wheel, and warns the driver about drops in tire pressure by illuminating the tire pressure warning light when the tire pressure drops below the threshold to reduce CO2 emissions and improve safety. Since tire pressure decreases over time, regular adjustments are necessary.


c.

A conventional type tire pressure warning valve and transmitters*1 and a high-pressure type tire pressure warning valve and transmitters*2 are used.

HINT:

*1: The conventional type tire pressure warning valve and transmitters can measure tire pressure up to approximately 380 kPa (55 psi, 3.8 bar).

*2: The high pressure type tire pressure warning valve and transmitters can measure tire pressure up to approximately 800 kPa (116 psi, 8.0 bar).



d.

The 5 tire pressure warning valve and transmitters each send the tire inflation pressure, temperature and ID code information to the tire pressure warning ECU and receiver.*

*: Models with full size spare tire (Models without a full size spare tire use 4 tire pressure warning valve and transmitters.)



e.

The tire pressure warning ECU and receiver, which consists of an antenna, a receiver and an ECU, monitors the tire pressure warning valve and transmitters to detect low tire pressure in each of the 5 tires including the spare tire.*

*: Models with full size spare tire (Models without a full size spare tire use 4 tires.)



f.

The operation of the tire pressure warning system includes a warning function, initial check function, initialization function and diagnosis function.


g.

The transmission cycle with which the information is sent from the tire pressure warning valve and transmitter to the pressure warning ECU and receiver is determined as follows based on the travel mode and the acceleration sensor built into each tire pressure warning valve and transmitter. Since the acceleration sensor is used only to determine if the vehicle is being driven, the value of rotational acceleration is not sent to the tire pressure warning ECU and receiver.

Travel Mode Transmission Cycle
While stopping*1 1 time / 1.5 min
While traveling*2 4 times / 1 min

HINT:

*1: When G that is applied to the acceleration sensor is less than 8 G.

*2: When G that is applied to the acceleration sensor is 8 G or greater.



h.

The warning threshold changes in accordance with pressure changes caused by driving conditions during initialization. Therefore, when a decreased tire pressure warning is displayed, the warning may not stop even though the tire inflation pressure has been adjusted to the recommended pressure. At that time, initialization is necessary.



PRECAUTION


a.

The system is disabled in the following conditions (when the condition becomes normal, the system will work properly):

i.

When all of the tires and wheels that have the tire pressure warning valve and transmitters registered with the tire pressure warning ECU and receiver have not been installed.


ii.

When all of the transmitter ID codes are not registered with the tire pressure warning ECU and receiver.


iii.

The conventional type tire pressure warning valve and transmitter:

When the tire pressure is extremely high [380 kPa (55 psi, 3.8 bar) or more].


iv.

The high pressure type tire pressure warning valve and transmitter:

When the tire pressure is extremely high [800 kPa (116 psi, 8.0 bar) or more].


v.

When the transmitter battery is depleted (battery life: approximately 10 years).



b.

The system may become disabled in the following conditions (when the condition becomes normal, the system will work properly):

i.

When electric devices or facilities using similar radio wave frequencies are nearby.


ii.

When a wireless device or other equipment operating at a similar frequency is in use in the vehicle.


iii.

When a window tint that affects the radio wave signals is installed.


iv.

When there is a lot of snow or ice on the vehicle, in particular around the wheels or wheel housings.


v.

When non-genuine wheels are used.


vi.

When tire chains are used.


vii.

When a tire repair sealant is used.


viii.

The spare tire is not within the receivable range of the tire pressure warning ECU and receiver.*

*: Models with full size spare tire



ix.

A large metallic object which can interfere with signal reception has been put in the cargo space.



c.

The tire pressure warning system may not function properly if a tire repair sealant has been used to repair a flat tire. If this type of sealant has been used, the tire pressure warning valve and transmitter should be replaced with a new one after repairing or replacing the tire.


d.

The tire pressure warning light may illuminate due to natural pressure loss caused by air passing through the tires or by changes in tire pressure caused by temperature fluctuations. When adjusting tire pressure, please be aware of the driving conditions and temperature conditions. The tire pressure warning light is prone to illuminate especially in late fall and during winter.
