1HZ EMISSION CONTROL  EMISSION CONTROL SYSTEM  CONTROL  EGR CONTROL  


FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS


a.

The main components of the EGR control are as follows:

Components Function
Emission Control ECU Controls the EGR control based on signals from each sensor.
EGR Valve Assembly Actuated by the emission control ECU via the vacuum regulating valve assembly and vacuum switching valve, this valve controls the flow rate of EGR gas.
Throttle Position Sensor Detects the amount of pedal effort applied to the accelerator pedal.
Engine Speed Sensor Detects the engine speed.
Engine Coolant Temperature Sensor Detects the engine coolant temperature.
Vacuum Sensor Detects the intake air pressure.
Vacuum Switching Valve Assembly (for EGR) Controls the vacuum that acts on the diaphragm of the EGR valve assembly according to signals from the emission control ECU.
Vacuum Regulating Valve Assembly



FUNCTION


a.

This system is designed to reduce and control NOx formation due to a slight reduction of the peak temperature in the engine combustion chamber, which is accomplished by introducing a small amount of inert gas into the intake manifold.


b.

By sensing the engine driving conditions, the emission control ECU electrically operates both the vacuum regulating valve assembly and vaccum switching valve assembly (for EGR valve), which controls the magnitude of vacuum introduced the into diaphragm of the EGR valve assembly.
