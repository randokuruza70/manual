FRONT SUSPENSION  FRONT SUSPENSION SYSTEM  DETAILS  SUSPENSION ARM  


CONSTRUCTION


a.

A forged leading arm is used to strengthen the durability aspect of the 4WD vehicle.


b.

An inter-ring bush with axial drillings is used for the lower arm bush on the frame side of the leading arm to ensure a full suspension stroke and improve drive performance on rough roads and riding comfort. Also, the inter-ring bush used for the control arm bush on the front axle housing side is optimally positioned so that when the vehicle rolls, an even larger roll stiffness is achieved using the bush reaction force.


c.

The span of the leading arm is lengthened and the frame side installation area is positioned lower than the center of the axle. These two characteristics suppress axle steer generated when turning and generate axle steer in the direction opposite of the steering direction to create understeer.
