INTRODUCTION  FEATURES  GENERAL  EQUIPMENT  


OUTLINE


a.

Winch

i.

A winch is provided for getting the vehicle out when it is stuck (at a standstill), when ascending or descending on steep roads, or when moving a load.



b.

Cruise Control System

i.

On models for Australia, the cruise control system is provided.



c.

Supplemental Restraint System

i.

The Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) driver and front passenger airbags are used as supplements to the seat belts to help reduce the shocks to the head and chest of the driver or front passenger in the event of a frontal collision.


ii.

On single cab models for Australia, the SRS driver knee airbag is used as a supplement to the seat belts to help restrain the driver's lower body (mainly knees), enhancing the effectiveness of driver protection.


iii.

On single cab models for Australia, SRS curtain shield airbags help to reduce shocks to the head of the driver and front passenger in the event of front and side collisions.
