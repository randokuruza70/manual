OPERATION


a.

Fuel Heater (Models with Fuel Heater)

i.

At low temperatures, the wax* separates during fueling and clogs the fuel filter element, increasing the negative pressure (vacuum) of the fuel past the fuel filter. When this increase in negative pressure (vacuum) reaches a predetermined value, the vacuum switch turns on, supplying current to the fuel heater and warming the fuel to melt the wax. When the wax melts and the negative pressure (vacuum) reaches a predetermined value, the vacuum switch turns off and stops supplying current to the fuel heater.

HINT:

*: Wax: At low temperatures, components in the fuel solidify, forming a wax-like compound.
