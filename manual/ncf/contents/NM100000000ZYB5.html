SUPPLEMENTAL RESTRAINT SYSTEMS  AIRBAG SYSTEM  GENERAL  GENERAL  


OUTLINE


a.

The Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) driver and front passenger* airbags are used as supplements to the seat belts to help to reduce shocks to the head and chest of the driver or front passenger in the event of a frontal collision.

*: Models with SRS front passenger airbag



b.

The SRS driver knee airbag is used as a supplement to the seat belts to help restrain the driver's lower body (mainly knees), enhancing the effectiveness of driver protection.*

*: Models with SRS driver knee airbag and SRS curtain shield airbag



c.

The SRS curtain shield airbags are used in the event of a frontal, side collision to help reduce shock to the head of the driver and front passenger.*

*: Models with SRS driver knee airbag and SRS curtain shield airbag

PRECAUTION


a.

Event Data Recorder (EDR) (Models with VSC)

i.

This vehicle is equipped with an Event Data Recorder (EDR). The main purpose of an EDR is to record data that will assist in understanding how vehicle systems have performed in certain crash or near-crash situations (for example, situations involving airbag deployment or collisions with a road obstacle). The EDR is designed to record data related to vehicle dynamics and safety systems for a short period of time, typically 30 seconds or less.


ii.

The EDR is designed to record such data as:

·

How various systems were operating

·

How far (if at all) the driver was depressing the accelerator and/or brake pedal

·

The vehicle speed



iii.

These data can be used to gain a better understanding of the circumstances in which crashes and injuries occur.

NOTICE:

Data are only recorded by the EDR in serious crash situations. No data are recorded by the EDR under normal driving conditions and no personal data (for example, name, gender, age and crash location) are recorded. However, other parties, such as law enforcement agencies, may be able to combine the EDR data with the type of personally identifying data routinely acquired during a crash investigation. To read data recorded by an EDR, special equipment is required, and access to the vehicle or the EDR is needed. In addition to the vehicle manufacturer, other parties which have the necessary special equipment, such as law enforcement agencies, will be able to read the information if they have access to the vehicle or the EDR.



iv.

Toyota will not disclose the data recorded in an EDR to a third party except when:

·

An agreement from the vehicle's owner (or the lessee for a leased vehicle) is obtained

·

Officially requested by the police or other authorities

·

For use by Toyota in a lawsuit

·

Ordered by a court of law



v.

However, if necessary, Toyota will:

·

Use the data for research on vehicle safety performance

·

Disclose the data to a third party for research purposes without disclosing information about the specific vehicle or vehicle owner




b.

Event Data Recorder (Models without VSC)

i.

There is a system to record data in a crash or near crash event. This is called an Event Data Recorder (EDR). The airbag sensor assembly contains the EDR.


ii.

In a crash or near crash event, this device may record some or all of the following information:

·

SRS airbag deployment data

·

SRS airbag system diagnostic data



iii.

The information above is intended to be used for the purpose of improving vehicle safety performance. Unlike general data recorders, the EDR does not record sound data such as conversation between passengers.


iv.

Toyota will not disclose the data recorded in an EDR to a third party except when:

·

An agreement from the vehicle's owner (or the leasing company for a leased vehicle) is obtained

·

Officially requested by the police or other authorities

·

Used as a defense for Toyota in a lawsuit

·

Ordered by the court



v.

However, if necessary, Toyota will:

·

Use the data for research on Toyota vehicle safety performance

·

Disclose the data to a third party for research purposes without disclosing details of the vehicle owner, and only when it is deemed necessary

·

Disclose summarized data cleared of vehicle identification information to a non-Toyota organization for research purposes
