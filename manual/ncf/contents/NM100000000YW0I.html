SYSTEM CONTROL


a.

Models Compliant with EURO 4 Emission Regulations

i.

In this system, the high-pressurized fuel that is supplied by the supply pump is stored in the common-rail, and the ECM sends signals to the injectors via the injector driver in order to control the injection timing and injection volume.


ii.

Fuel pressure is controlled in accordance with the fuel pumping volume that is controlled by the Suction Control Valve (SCV) in the supply pump assembly. The SCV is controlled by the ECM.

b.

Models Compliant with EURO 5 Emission Regulations

i.

In this system, the high-pressurized fuel that is supplied by the supply pump assembly is stored in the common-rail assembly, and the ECM sends signals to the injector assemblies via the injector driver in order to control the injection timing and injection volume.


ii.

A common-rail assembly with the fuel pressure discharge valve and piezo type injector assembly with maximum injection pressure of 200 MPa are used to improve driveability and reduce Noise and Vibration (NV).


iii.

Fuel pressure is controlled in accordance with the fuel pumping volume that is controlled by the Suction Control Valve (SCV) in the supply pump assembly and fuel pressure discharge valve. The SCV is controlled by the ECM. The fuel discharge valve is controlled by the ECM via the injector driver.
