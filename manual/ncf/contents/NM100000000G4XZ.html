STEERING COLUMN  STEERING COLUMN SYSTEM  DETAILS  MANUAL TILT AND TELESCOPIC MECHANISM  


CONSTRUCTION


a.

The manual tilt and telescopic mechanism allows adjustment of the steering position in the vertical and longitudinal (extending and retracting) directions through lever operation. This provides the driver with the most comfortable driving position.


b.

A structure that allows operation from a single steering tilt lever is used to simplify the structure and improve operability.


c.

The tilt and telescopic mechanism consists of a steering tilt lever, No. 1 tilt lever lock bolt, No. 1 tilt steering adjusting nut, steering column tube stoppers, break away bracket, steering column upper tube, steering column lower tube and other components.


d.

The stepless tilt mechanism enables the steering column to be tilted up to 3.2Â°, and the stepless telescopic mechanism enables the steering column to be moved up to 40 mm (1.57 in.).


e.

When the tilt and telescopic mechanism is in its locked state, the steering tilt lever at the lock position causes the cam of the steering column tube stopper to tighten the attachment of break away bracket. This secures the break away bracket, column bracket and steering column upper tube, so movement in the tilt direction and in the telescopic direction is locked.


f.

When the tilt and telescopic mechanism is in its free state, the steering tilt lever at the free position causes the cam of the steering column tube stoppers to loosen the attachment of break away bracket. This frees the steering column upper tube to move in the tilt direction and in the telescopic direction, enabling adjustment.
