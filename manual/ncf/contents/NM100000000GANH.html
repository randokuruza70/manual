AXLE AND DIFFERENTIAL  AXLE SYSTEM  DETAILS  DUAL MODE AUTOMATIC LOCKING HUB  


CONSTRUCTION


a.

The dual mode automatic locking hub consists of a slide gear, drive gear, shift spring, return spring, retainer, knob, cam spring, cam, outer brake, inner brake, brake wire, release plate and housing.

i.

The drive force of the drive shaft is transmitted to the housing due to the engagement of the slide gear and drive gear.


ii.

The slide gear slides to engage the drive gear due to the operation of the knob, retainer, return spring and shift spring.


iii.

The cam rotates due to the operation of the outer brake, inner brake, brake wire and release plate.

OPERATION


a.

AUTO Position

i.

Free→Lock Operation

·

When switching the drive mode from H2 to H4 or L4, the drive gear and cam rotate due to the rotation of the drive shaft.

·

The outer brake is secured to the spindle. The cam moves the cam face of the outer brake while compressing the return spring.

·

The slide gear engages with the spline of the drive gear due to the movement of the cam. At this time, the inner brake is pressed into the cam and rotates. However, when the lug position of the inner brake contacts the inside of the brake wire opening, the brake operates due to the brake wire diameter expanding and contacting the inner surface of the outer brake and the rotation of the inner brake stops.

ii.

Lock→Free Operation

·

When the vehicle is driven in 4WD (either forward or in reverse) and the drive mode is switched from H4 or L4 to H2, and then the vehicle is driven in the opposite direction, the lug portion of the inner brake contacts the inside of the brake wire opening and the brake operates to stop the rotation of the inner brake.

·

The cam moves the cam face of the inner brake and outer brake.

·

The slide gear moves due to the force of the return spring and disengages from the drive gear, allowing the hub to become free.
