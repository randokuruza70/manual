STEERING COLUMN  STEERING COLUMN SYSTEM  DETAILS  ENERGY ABSORBING MECHANISM  


CONSTRUCTION


a.

The energy absorbing mechanism mainly consists of a No .2 steering intermediate shaft sub-assembly, break away bracket, break away capsule and steering column upper tube and steering column lower tube.


b.

The steering column upper tube is mounted on the instrument panel reinforcement via the break away capsule and break away bracket. The steering column assembly and the power steering gear assembly are connected with a contractile No. 2 steering intermediate shaft sub-assembly.



OPERATION


a.

Primary Collision

i.

When the power steering gear assembly moves in toward the cabin during a collision (primary collision), the steering main shaft assembly and No. 2 steering intermediate shaft assembly contract in the axis direction and the corrugated shaft section of the No. 2 steering intermediate shaft assembly bends and deforms in order to absorb impact, thus helping prevent the steering column assembly and the steering wheel assembly from moving into the cabin.

Secondary Collision

i.

When an impact is transmitted to the steering wheel assembly during a collision (secondary collision), the break away bracket, which is secured to the instrument panel reinforcement through the break away capsule, detaches and moves forward. At this time, the sliding resistance between the steering column upper tube and steering column lower tube contributes to the reduction of impact energy while ensuring a survival space for the driver.
