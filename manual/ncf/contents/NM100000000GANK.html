SYSTEM CONTROL


a.

The No. 2 4WD control ECU operates the differential lock shift actuators and differential lock indicator lights in accordance with the conditions of the differential lock control switch (center differential lock switch) and other switches. If the vehicle speed goes above 8 km/h (5 mph), the No. 2 4WD control ECU prohibits shifting of the differential lock shift actuator from the FREE mode to the LOCK mode. The operation of the No. 2 4WD control ECU is as follows:

i.

Shifting from FREE Mode to LOCK Mode

·

When all the following conditions are satisfied, the No. 2 4WD control ECU drives the differential lock shift motor and restricts operation of the differential:

·

The differential lock control switch (center differential lock switch) is in the RR or FR/RR position.

·

The 4WD detection switch (No. 1 transfer indicator switch) is on.

·

The vehicle speed is 8 km/h (5 mph) or lower.


·

When the differential lock control switch (center differential lock switch) is turned to the RR position, only the rear differential is shifted to the LOCK mode.

·

When the differential lock control switch (center differential lock switch) is turned to the FR/RR position, both the front and rear differentials are shifted to the LOCK mode.



ii.

Shifting from LOCK Mode to FREE Mode

·

When one of the following conditions is satisfied, the No. 2 4WD control ECU drives the differential lock shift motor and releases the restriction on operation of the differential.

·

The differential lock control switch (center differential lock switch) is turned to the OFF position.

·

The differential lock control switch (center differential lock switch) is turned from the FR/RR position to the RR position.*

·

The 4WD detection switch (No. 1 transfer indicator switch) is turned off.

HINT:

*: When this occurs, only the front differential is shifted to FREE mode.





iii.

Control of Differential Lock Indicator Light

·

The No. 2 4WD control ECU turns off, blinks or turns on the differential lock indicator lights in accordance with the conditions of the differential lock control switch (center differential lock switch) and differential lock position switches (No. 4 transfer indicator switches) as shown in the following table.

·
