a.

Upper Body

i.

The body has low vibration and noise due to the high rigidity provided by reinforcements positioned at the connections of each pillar.


ii.

Reinforcements are positioned in the side portion of the upper body at the connections of each pillar and the roof side rail. Also, reinforcements are positioned at the connections of each pillar and the rocker.


iii.

Reinforcements are provided in the floor at the connections of the dash panel and the floor, and the dash panel and each pillar.

b.

Frame

i.

4 types of frames are used depending on the wheel base.

Short Wheel Base (The illustration shown is an example only.)

Semi-long Wheel Base (The illustration shown is an example only.)

Long Wheel Base (The illustration shown is an example only.)

Super Long Wheel Base (The illustration shown is an example only.)


ii.

On single cab models for Australia, a large side rail cross section is provided for the frame, and reinforced members with ultra high strength sheet steel (tensile strength: 980 MPa) are positioned effectively to ensure superior collision safety.


iii.

On single cab models for Australia, the steering gear box position of the frame is moved backwards to ensure the axial compression length of the front part of the side rail. As a result, sufficient energy absorption is performed in the front part of the rail until the frame begins to bend, ensuring a survival space in the cabin.


iv.

On single cab models for Australia, the cross member is added and the cross section of the side rail is optimized for the frame to control the frame bending mode during a collision, ensuring a survival space in the cabin.
